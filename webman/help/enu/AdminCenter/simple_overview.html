Overview

Control Panel (Simple Mode) > Overview is a quick and centralized gateway that allows you to manage various basic settings and monitor system resources of your DiskStation.

System Health

This section displays the overall status of your system. If an error has occurred or your storage spaces have crashed or degraded, you can hover your mouse over this section and click the More icon on the top-right corner to open corresponding pages for repair and management.

DSM Version

Your current DSM version and status will be shown in this section. When a new DSM is available for download, you can update to the latest version directly from here.

QuickConnect ID

You can view your QuickConnect ID here. For more advanced settings and management, hover your mouse over this section and click the More icon on the top-right corner to open the QuickConnect page.

Storage

This section displays the hardware information and status of your storage spaces, such as hard disk utilization and volume usage/capacity. You can check how many hard drives are being used by what storage spaces. For more advanced settings and management, hover your mouse over this section and click the More icon on the top-right corner to open Storage Manager.

Resource Monitor

You can monitor the percentage of the overall CPU usage, memory usage, and the data sent and received rate in KB per second. If more than one LAN is in use, click on the arrow icon on the network flow chart to switch. For more advanced settings and management, hover your mouse over this section and click the More icon on the top-right corner to open Resource Monitor.

Connected Users

You can view and manage the clients who are currently connected to the system and accessing resources here. This section displays clients that are connected via HTTP, TELNET, SSH, SMB/CIFS, AFP, FTP and NFS. For more advanced settings and management, hover your mouse over this section and click the More icon on the top-right corner to open Resource Monitor.

To kill current connections:

Click the Kill connection icon next to the desired user to stop the user from accessing the resource. The disconnected user has to be authorized again to gain access to the resource.
