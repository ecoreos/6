Info Center

Info Center displays basic information regarding various aspects of your DiskStation, such as hardware specifications, network, storage, and services. Info Center is located at Control Panel > Info Center.

General

The General tab displays the basic information regarding your DiskStation, such as serial number, model name, the amount of physical memory, DSM version, time information, thermal status, or external devices.

Thermal Status*

The thermal status of the DiskStation is indicated by green or orange lights:

  • Green: Thermal status is normal.
  • Orange: Temperature of the DiskStation is too high. If the temperature of the DiskStation remains high, it might shut down due to overheating.

Network

The Network tab displays network information, such as DNS server, IP address, subnet mask, and more.

Storage

The Storage tab displays information related to volume usage and hard disk status.

The Storage tab displays information related to storage usage and the status of external storage devices.

Hard Disk Status

The following are the possible status types of an internal hard disk:

  • No Disk Installed: No disk is installed in the corresponding hard disk slot.
  • Not Initialized: System partition and volume are not established on the hard disk.
  • Normal: System partition and volume are available for use on the hard disk.
  • Initialized: System partition is established, but no volume has been created.
  • System Partition Failed: System partition on the hard disk is unavailable.
  • Crashed: Failed to access volume on the hard disk. We recommend that you replace the hard disk.

Note:

For the system to function normally, there should be at least one disk in Normal or Initialized status.

Service

The Service tab displays a list of services and installed packages, including options to enable or disable each service and package. In addition, for some services and packages, there are options to test connectivity or choose whether or not each service or package can be accessed through the firewall.

To modify service or package settings:

  1. The following options are available:
    • Enable: Enable or disable the services or package.
    • Allow through firewall: Add or remove the ports used by the service or application at Control Panel > External Access > Router Configuration, allowing the service or package to pass through the firewall of the DiskStation.
    • Settings: Select the network interface whose firewall settings you would like to display and manage. You can select All to display and manage the firewall settings of all network interfaces.
    • Test Connection: Test the ports used by services and packages. This allows you to test whether or not devices can access the services and package from an external network.
  2. Click Save to save changes or Reset to discard changes.

Synology Account

Synology Account is a well-integrated web platform that helps users access the following services offered by Synology:

  • The QuickConnect and DDNS services for your DiskStation
  • Software license management
  • Synology Urgent Support
  • eNews subscription
  • Purchase history of Synology products

Note:

You may also log in to your Synology Account with a social network account (Facebook, Google+, and LinkedIn).

To register or log in to your Synology Account:

  1. Go to Control Panel > Info Center > Synology Account.
  2. Click Log in to or register a Synology Account.
  3. Specify the user credentials or click Sign up to register a Synology Account. Alternatively, you may log in with a social network account.

To manage your Synology Account:

  1. Click Manage Account to reach the web platform.
  2. Follow the web instructions to access your Synology Account for management.

To log out of your Synology Account:

Click Change Account to log out of your Synology Account.

Note:

Logging out of your Synology Account may have the associated services from Synology (e.g. QuickConnect and DDNS) suspended.
