Overview

You can check current the status of DSM, scan the system, and check the scanning progress and result summary. Security Advisor scans DSM and your DiskStation by checking a number of security rules. The number and types of rules depend on how you configure your Security Baseline. Your Security Baseline is determined by your needs. Are you using your DiskStation for work and business or home and personal use? You will be asked to choose a Security Baseline the first time you start a scan. This can be changed later in the Advanced page.

System status

Security Advisor has a total of seven statuses. Once a scan is finished, you will see a results summary across five categories. The different statuses include:

  • Ready to scan: This status is displayed when you have not scanned your DiskStation yet. Click Scan to start scanning.
  • Scanning: A scan is in progress. You can view the the progress of the scan. Click Stop to stop scanning.
  • Good: The current scan has finished. Security Advisor found no weak settings or security risks. Your DiskStation is currently cafe.
  • Warning: The current scan has finished. Due to some vulnerable security settings, Security Advisor reported some low/moderate security risks. You are advised to take the recommended actions to make your DiskStation safer.
  • At Risk: The current scan has finished. Due to some vulnerable security settings, Security Advisor reported some high security risks. You are strongly advised to take the recommended actions to make your DiskStation safer.
  • Danger: The current scan has finished. Security Advisor found malware on your DiskStation. You must take the recommended actions to prevent any further undesired consequences to your DiskStation.
  • Out-of-date: Your DSM version is out-of-date. It is recommended you update DSM to receive the newest features, enhancements, and security fixes to to improve your experience and keep your DiskStation safe.

Security Advisor groups checks into five categories:

  • Malware: Detect whether malware exists on your DiskStation and if your DiskStation is under attack
  • System: Detect system-related security settings.
  • Account: Detect account and password security settings.
  • Network: Detect network-related security settings.
  • Update: Detect whether DSM and other installed packages are up-to-date, as well as check update-related settings.

Once a scan has finished, you can view detailed results on a report-by-report basis by clicking View Results.
